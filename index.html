Who's to blame for Staten Island Ferry service issues?

The Staten Island Ferry, once regarded as one of the most reliable forms of mass transit in New York City, has fallen into disarray in recent years. The Department of Transportation's inability to recruit and retain qualified crew members during a national maritime workers shortage has resulted in regular service disruptions, particularly during afternoon and overnight hours. The Marine Engineers Beneficial Association, which represents a sizable portion of the Ferry's workforce, has argued that New York City's continued refusal to provide crew members with a “fair and respectful” new contract has contributed to the staffing issues, and subsequently, the service disruptions.

While these two parties have been front and center,
there are various others playing a role in the ongoing issues.
